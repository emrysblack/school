Solution to Exercise 4 on page 275

\left ( \frac{1}{m} \right )n * {m} = \frac{1}{mn-1}

1/m is the probability of hitting a specific bucket. We raise to the n for the probability of hitting every time. We then multiply it by m to extend it to any single bucket.

Solution to Exercise 3 on page 279

Following the technique outlined in the book, I used the formula

\left ⌊ log{_{\left ⌈ m/2 \right ⌉}\frac{n+1}{4}} \right ⌋ + 1

with 100 million for n, and got the height from plugging in values for m in wolfram alpha. I tried going up by 100 each time until I hit 600. I then tested 550, 580, 590, 585, 583, and 584 for m. This allowed me to discover that 585 was the minimum order for the desired height of 3.
